Expert Advice

Quick Construction Tips
from Our Engineers

01

Use M20 Concrete (Minimum) for All RCC Work

Many contractors use M15 to cut costs. For any structural member — slab, beam, column — always insist on minimum M20 grade. The cost difference is small; the safety difference is significant.

02

Brick Walls Need a 1:6 Cement-Sand Mortar

A common contractor shortcut is using 1:8 or weaker mortar to save cement. A proper 1:6 cement:sand ratio ensures wall strength. Insist on this during plastering and brickwork supervision.

03

Cure Concrete for Minimum 28 Days

Concrete achieves only 70% of its design strength at 7 days. Full curing requires 28 days of moisture. Many structural failures happen because contractors skip proper curing to speed up work.

04

Check TMT Bar Diameter Before Pouring

Always verify that the steel reinforcement bars match the structural drawing specifications before pouring concrete. Substituting thinner bars is a common (and dangerous) contractor shortcut.
